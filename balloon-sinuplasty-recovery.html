What to Expect

Understanding Your Recovery

Since the treatment is non-invasive with no stitches and no nasal packing, your balloon sinuplasty recovery should be quick. After your procedure, you will experience minimal discomfort and should follow easy-to-do post-procedure guidance.

Your nose and throat area may feel numb for a few hours after your procedure before the local anesthesia wears off. Despite this sensation, you will still be able to breathe and swallow.

  • Try not to blow your nose for the first 24–48 hours
  • Keep nasal passages moist with saline sprays and rinses several times a day
  • Some light bleeding may occur the first day after your procedure
  • If you need to sneeze, release it through your mouth
  • Resume activities within 48 hours
  • After one week, your sinuses will be 90% healed

Common Questions

Balloon Sinuplasty FAQs

Can balloon sinuplasty be used for children?

Yes! Balloon sinuplasty can be a safe and effective treatment option for pediatric patients with chronic sinusitis. Our specialists evaluate each child individually to determine if BSP is the right approach.

Is balloon sinuplasty a permanent solution?

Yes! The results of balloon sinuplasty are long-lasting. The procedure restructures the sinus openings, and the vast majority of patients experience lasting relief from chronic sinus symptoms.

Does balloon sinuplasty limit future treatment options?

No! Balloon sinuplasty does not remove any bone or tissue, so it preserves your anatomy and does not limit any future treatment options should they be needed.
