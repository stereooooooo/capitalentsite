Understanding Dizziness

Do You Experience Episodes of Dizziness or Imbalance?

If you do, you are not alone. Dizziness and imbalance is the second most common reason someone visits their family doctor. Determining the cause of your dizziness requires a thorough evaluation of your balance system.

The team of experts at Capital ENT and Sinus Center, including ENT physicians and audiologists, will perform a comprehensive evaluation of the ear and balance system and coordinate your care with other specialists as necessary, such as cardiology or neurology.

Do your symptoms include:

  • Lightheadedness
  • Feeling off balance
  • Vertigo (the illusion that your surroundings are moving)
  • Unsteadiness when walking or standing

How We Diagnose

Advanced Diagnostic Testing

Capital ENT uses state-of-the-art technology to pinpoint the cause of your dizziness and create a targeted treatment plan.

In-Office CT Scanner

Our physicians use advanced diagnostic imaging with our in-office CT scanner to provide high-resolution pictures of the middle ear cavity and surrounding structures that may be contributing to your dizziness.

Comprehensive Hearing Tests

Our audiologists use the latest in sound isolation booth technology to obtain highly accurate hearing testing. This evaluation is critical as many causes of dizziness may also cause hearing loss.

Videonystagmography (VNG)

Our audiologists may perform a VNG test, which evaluates the different parts of the balance system to determine which part is causing the problem and guide targeted treatment.

MRI Imaging

When needed, our physicians may order an outpatient MRI to check for abnormalities of the brain or inner ear structures that may be causing dizziness or balance problems.

Our Approach

A Team Approach to Your Balance Health

Dizziness can stem from many different causes — the inner ear, the brain, the cardiovascular system, or other conditions. That's why our approach starts with a thorough evaluation by both our ENT physicians and audiologists working together.

Once we identify the source of your balance problem, we develop a personalized treatment plan. When appropriate, we coordinate your care with other specialists such as cardiologists or neurologists to ensure you receive the most comprehensive care possible.

  • Board-certified ENT physicians with balance disorder expertise
  • Licensed audiologists performing advanced vestibular testing
  • On-site state-of-the-art hearing and balance testing labs
  • Coordinated care with cardiology, neurology, and other specialties
  • Same-day and next-day appointments often available
