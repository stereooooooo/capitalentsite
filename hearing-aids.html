Styles We Fit

Hearing Aid Styles for Every Ear & Lifestyle

We fit all major hearing aid styles. Our audiologist will help you choose the best style based on your degree of hearing loss, ear anatomy, dexterity, and cosmetic preferences.

Behind-the-Ear

BTE

The device sits behind the ear with a thin tube or earmold connecting into the ear canal. Durable, easy to handle, and powerful enough for all degrees of hearing loss.

Best for: All ages, all degrees of hearing loss, those who need easy handling

Receiver-in-Canal

RIC / RITE

A small device behind the ear sends sound through a thin wire to a receiver placed directly in the ear canal. Near-invisible, excellent sound quality, and a natural listening experience.

Best for: Mild to severe loss; most popular style for first-time wearers

In-the-Ear

ITE

Custom-molded to fill the outer portion of your ear bowl. Larger controls make it easier to adjust, and the custom fit is comfortable all day. Directional microphones and Bluetooth available.

Best for: Mild to severe loss; users who prefer easy-to-handle controls

In-the-Canal

ITC

Smaller than ITE, custom-molded to sit partially in the ear canal. Less visible than ITE, comfortable, and can include directional microphones and volume controls.

Best for: Mild to moderately-severe hearing loss; cosmetic-conscious wearers

Completely-in-Canal

CIC

Fits entirely inside the ear canal and is nearly invisible from the outside. Custom-molded for a comfortable, secure fit. Ideal for mild to moderate hearing loss.

Best for: Mild to moderate loss; maximum discretion

Invisible-in-Canal

IIC

The smallest, most discreet hearing aid available. Sits deep in the ear canal and is completely invisible when worn. Leverages the ear's natural shape for a more natural sound experience.

Best for: Mild to moderate loss; those who want total invisibility

Bone-Anchored

BAHA

Transmits sound vibrations through the skull bone directly to the cochlea — bypassing the outer and middle ear. Available as a surgically implanted device or a non-surgical softband/headband system.

Best for: Conductive hearing loss, single-sided deafness, microtia/atresia, or when conventional aids aren't suitable. Ask Dr. Sullivan at Lakeway.

The Fitting Journey

What to Expect at Your Hearing Aid Appointment

Our process is thorough, unhurried, and focused entirely on your hearing goals. Most patients leave their fitting appointment hearing sounds they haven't heard in years.

1

Diagnostic Hearing Test

A full audiometric evaluation in our on-site sound-treated booth identifies the type, degree, and configuration of your hearing loss. Takes about 45–60 minutes.

2

Lifestyle Consultation

We discuss your daily listening environments, communication priorities, cosmetic preferences, dexterity needs, and budget to narrow down the best options for you.

3

Fitting & Verification

Your hearing aids are programmed and verified using Real Ear Measurements — a probe microphone placed in your ear canal confirms the hearing aid is delivering the right amount of sound at every frequency.

4

Follow-Up & Fine-Tuning

Follow-up visits at 2 weeks and 6 weeks allow us to adjust programming as your brain adjusts, answer questions, and ensure your hearing aids are performing optimally.

Common Questions

Hearing Aid FAQ

Common signs include frequently asking people to repeat themselves, difficulty understanding speech in noisy environments, turning up the TV louder than others prefer, and missing high-pitched sounds like doorbells or birds. A diagnostic audiological evaluation at Capital ENT will determine the degree and type of your hearing loss and whether hearing aids are recommended. Since some hearing loss has a treatable medical cause, we always recommend a full evaluation before purchasing any device.
Coverage varies by plan. Some insurance plans, including many Medicare Advantage plans, cover hearing aids partially or in full. Traditional Medicare (Part B) does not cover hearing aids, but covers diagnostic hearing testing when ordered by a physician. Our team will verify your specific benefits before your appointment so there are no surprises. We also offer flexible financing through CareCredit and accept HSA/FSA funds.
We fit all major brands including Phonak, Oticon, Starkey, Signia, ReSound, Widex, Unitron, and Bernafon. Our audiologist selects the best brand and model based on your specific hearing loss, lifestyle, and preferences — not brand contracts or sales quotas. If you have a preference for a specific brand based on prior experience, just let us know.
Most patients need 4–6 weeks to fully adjust to hearing aids. Your brain must relearn how to process sounds it has been missing — a phenomenon called auditory acclimatization. You may find voices sound "tinny" at first, or that everyday sounds like footsteps seem loud. This is normal and temporary. We schedule follow-up appointments at 2 weeks and 6 weeks to make any necessary adjustments to programming, fit, and volume settings.
Over-the-counter (OTC) hearing aids are designed for self-fitting by adults with mild to moderate hearing loss, without professional involvement. Prescription hearing aids, fitted by our audiologist, are programmed precisely to your audiogram using Real Ear Measurement verification and cover a much wider range of hearing loss severities. A professional fitting also includes the medical evaluation to identify any treatable underlying condition — something no OTC device provides.
Most hearing aids last 5–7 years with proper care. Daily maintenance includes wiping the devices clean of earwax and moisture and storing them in a dry case overnight. We provide professional cleaning and minor repairs at your annual follow-up visits. Wax filters and domes (small tips that go in the ear) are replaced every few months and are inexpensive. Rechargeable models eliminate the need for weekly battery changes.

Early intervention is critical for children with hearing loss to support normal speech, language, and cognitive development.
