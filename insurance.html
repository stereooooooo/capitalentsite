Do You Need a Referral?

PPO vs. HMO: Know Before You Go

Whether you need a referral depends entirely on your insurance plan type. Here's a quick breakdown.

PPO Plans

No Referral Needed

Preferred Provider Organization (PPO) plans allow you to see any in-network specialist directly — no referral from your primary care doctor required.

  • Schedule directly with Capital ENT
  • Lower cost when using in-network providers
  • Can also see out-of-network providers at higher cost
  • Examples: most BCBS, Aetna, Cigna, UHC PPO plans
HMO Plans

Referral Required

Health Maintenance Organization (HMO) plans require a referral from your Primary Care Physician (PCP) before you can see a specialist like an ENT.

  • Call your PCP first to request an ENT referral
  • PCP submits referral to your insurance
  • Once approved, call Capital ENT to schedule
  • Examples: most HMO, Medicaid managed care plans

Not sure which plan type you have? Check the back of your insurance card — it will typically say "PPO," "HMO," or "EPO." You can also call the member services number on your card, or call us at 512-339-4040 and we'll help you figure it out.

Before Your Visit

How to Verify Your Benefits

Understanding your coverage before your appointment prevents surprises. Here's exactly what to do.

1

Call Us First

Contact our billing team at 512-339-4040. Give us your insurance card information and we'll verify your in-network status, deductible, and estimated co-pay before you arrive.

2

Check Your Card

While we verify, check the back of your insurance card for the member services phone number. Ask them: "Is Capital ENT in-network?" and "What is my specialist co-pay and deductible?"

3

Get Your Referral (if needed)

If you have an HMO plan, contact your primary care physician to request a referral to Capital ENT. Bring or have the referral number sent to us before your appointment.

4

Bring Your Card

Bring your insurance card and a photo ID to your appointment. If your insurance or address has changed recently, let us know when you arrive so we can update your records.

New Patients

What to Bring to Your Appointment

Having the right documents ready speeds up check-in and helps our team verify your benefits on the spot.

Insurance Card(s)

Bring all current insurance cards, including any secondary insurance. Front and back — we'll need the member ID and group number.

Photo ID

A valid driver's license or government-issued ID is required for all patients, including minors (parent or guardian ID).

Referral Paperwork

If your HMO requires a referral, bring the referral form or authorization number. Without it, your visit may not be covered.

Medication List

Bring a current list of all medications and dosages, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements. This helps your provider give safe, accurate care.

Imaging & Records

If you have prior CT scans, MRIs, or hearing tests related to your visit, bring them on disc or as digital files. This prevents duplicate testing.

Co-pay Payment

Co-pays are due at the time of service. We accept cash, checks, and all major credit cards. If you have questions about your estimated co-pay, call us before your visit.

Billing & Insurance FAQ

You may receive a bill from Capital ENT for the physician's professional fee, and a separate bill from the facility (e.g., a surgery center or hospital) if your procedure was performed outside our office. This is standard practice and both bills are legitimate. If you have questions about either, call our billing office at 512-339-4040.
Prior authorization (also called pre-auth or pre-certification) is approval from your insurance company before a procedure or service is performed. Many surgeries and some diagnostic tests require it. We handle the prior authorization process on your behalf — you don't need to call your insurance separately. We'll notify you when authorization is received and provide your estimated out-of-pocket costs before scheduling.
Don't panic — claim denials are common and often reversible. First, call our billing team at 512-339-4040 so we can review the denial reason. Most denials are due to missing documentation, coding issues, or prior auth requirements we can resolve. We will appeal on your behalf when appropriate and guide you through your options.
You can pay your bill securely online through our online payment portal. You can also pay by phone at 512-339-4040, by mail, or in person at any of our four office locations. We accept cash, check, and all major credit cards.
Your deductible is the amount you must pay out-of-pocket before your insurance starts covering costs. For example, if you have a $1,500 deductible and haven't met it yet, you'll owe the contracted rate for your visit until you reach $1,500 for the year. After that, you typically pay only a co-pay or co-insurance. Deductibles reset each January 1 for most plans. Our billing team can check your deductible status when you call to schedule.
