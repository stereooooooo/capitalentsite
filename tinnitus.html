Understanding Tinnitus

Do You Suffer From Tinnitus?

Tinnitus is a condition in which a person perceives sound that is not actually present in the environment. In most cases, tinnitus is related to damage in the auditory system, which may or may not include hearing loss. Sometimes a single event, a medication, or a condition unrelated to the hearing system can trigger tinnitus.

Tinnitus can significantly affect quality of life — interfering with concentration, sleep, and daily activities. The good news is that it can almost always be treated, and symptoms can be improved through personalized care.

Do your symptoms include:

  • Ringing in the ears
  • Hissing or buzzing sounds
  • Whooshing or pulsing noises
  • Sounds that interfere with sleep or concentration

How We Treat Tinnitus

Personalized Sound Therapy

Tinnitus can almost always be treated and its symptoms can be improved, in most cases through sound therapy that can be customized for each patient's needs. This technology is available through many of today's advanced hearing aids.

Our audiologists — Margaux Colburn, AuD (Austin) and Jill Sullivan, AuD, CCC-A (Lakeway) — work alongside our board-certified otolaryngologists to identify any medical contributors to tinnitus before developing your personalized sound therapy plan.

  • Comprehensive audiological and tinnitus evaluation
  • Identification of underlying medical conditions
  • Customized sound therapy through advanced hearing aids
  • Ongoing follow-up care and treatment adjustments
  • Coordination with ENT physicians when needed
